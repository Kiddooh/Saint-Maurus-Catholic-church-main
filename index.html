Our Mission & Vision

Saint Maurus Catholic Church is dedicated to fostering a vibrant community of faith where individuals and families can grow in their relationship with Jesus Christ. Our mission is to celebrate the sacraments, proclaim the Gospel, serve those in need, and build up the Body of Christ.

We envision a church where every member is a disciple who makes disciples, transforming our community and world through the power of the Holy Spirit.

Our History

Founded in 1895 by a small group of immigrant families, Saint Maurus Catholic Church has been a beacon of faith and hope in our community for over 125 years. Our beautiful church building, constructed in 1923, stands as a testament to the dedication and sacrifice of those who came before us.

Throughout our history, we have remained committed to serving our community through times of joy and challenge, always guided by the teachings of Christ and the rich traditions of the Catholic faith.

Our Beliefs

The Trinity

We believe in one God in three Persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Sacred Scripture

The Bible is the inspired Word of God and the authoritative guide for our faith.

The Church

The Catholic Church is the Body of Christ, founded by Jesus to continue His mission.

Sacraments

We celebrate seven sacraments as visible signs of God's grace in our lives.

New Here? Plan Your Visit

We're excited to welcome you to Saint Maurus Catholic Church! Here's what you can expect when you visit:

1

When to Arrive

Plan to arrive about 10-15 minutes before Mass to find parking and a seat. Our greeters will welcome you and provide any assistance you need.

2

What to Wear

Come as you are! While some dress more formally, you'll find people in everything from suits to casual attire. We care more about you than what you wear.

3

Children Welcome

We love having children in our worship services! We offer a nursery for infants and toddlers, and children's liturgy during the 10:00 AM Mass.

4

After Mass

Join us for coffee and fellowship after each Sunday Mass. It's a great opportunity to meet people and learn more about our parish community.
